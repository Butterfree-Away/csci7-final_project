CSCI E-7 Final Project May 2020

Facebook Controversies of the Last Decade: An Overview

By Jamal Merritt

How can we better understand Facebook controversies - specifically those related to war crimes, violence, and harmful data practices - over the past 10 years?
This project aims to answer that question through using Python's matplotlib, pandas, and numpy libraries to construct a visual representation of Facebook's political mishaps over the last decade. Using a self- created csv file* [composed a sample of 50 randomly selected controversial events involving Facebook within the last decade], three key figures were generated for use in the project: a scatterplot depicting the number of major political Facebook Controversies over the last year, a timeline generated via a stemline plot, and a table of values.




Figure 1: Scatter Plot of Number of Major Controversies Per Year

This scatterplot depicts the relationship between the frequency of controversies and the year. As evident in the plot, the number of controversies that Facebook has been involved in has increased overall in recent years. The largest increase in the number of reported events from this sample was in 2018 ( a growth of 5). The infamous Facebook Cambridge Analytica scandal occured during that year.




Figure 2: Timeline of Facebook Controversies in the Last Decade

This timeline stemplot is a visual representation of controversies involving Facebook from 2010 - 2020. A large number of the events cluster within the August 2018 - July 2019 period.

Kurdish Opposition Censorship 2013-08-24 2013 Facebook has a policy to censor anything related to Kurdish opposition against Turkey, such as maps of Kurdistan, flags of Kurdish armed groups (such as PKK and YPG), and criticism of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, the founder of Turkey.

PRISM Surveillance Program 2013-06-08 2013 The 2013 mass surveillance disclosures identified Facebook as a participant in the U.S. National Security Administration's PRISM program.
Updated Hate Speech Policies 2013-05-29 2013 A series of pro-rape and 'rape joke' content on Facebook drew attention from the media and women's groups. Facebook agreed to take action after it had "become clear that our systems to identify and remove hate speech have failed to work as effectively as we would like, particularly around issues of gender-based hate".
Fake Accounts 2012-08-03 2012 Facebook revealed that more than 83 million Facebook accounts (8.7% of total users) are fake accounts. These fake profiles consist of duplicate profiles, accounts for spamming purposes and personal profiles for business, organization or non-human entities such as pets.

Lawsuit for Privacy Violations 2012-05-18 2012 Law firm Stewarts Law US combined 21 privacy lawsuits against the social network into a single, class-action suit for $15 billion, charging the world's largest social network with violating user privacy by allegedly tracking their Web usage.
Breastfeeding Photos 2011-12-30 2011 Facebook removed photos of mothers breastfeeding and after public criticism, restored the photos.
Tracking Cookies 2011-09-25 2011 Austrian internet security blogger, Nik Cubrilovic, successfully forced Facebook to address a privacy flaw whereby the site would continue to track your web surfing habits even when logged out.
Investigation by the IDPC 2011-08-01 2011 The Irish Data Protection Commissioner led an investigation on Facebook after receiving 22 complaints by a group of Austrian students. In spring 2012, Facebook had to undertake many privacy policy changes.
Anti-Google Smear Campaign 2011-05-12 2011 Facebook paid for an anti-Google email campain sent to journalists and bloggers making critical allegations about Google's privacy policies.
Young v. Facebook, Inc. 2011-05-09 2011 In Young v. Facebook, Inc., plaintiff Karen Beth Young alleged violations of the Americans with Disabilities Act and related state laws on disability as well as breach of contract and negligence. A District Court judge dismissed the complaint, ruling that Facebook is a website, not a physical place, so the Americans with Disabilities Act does not apply.
Fraley v. Facebook, Inc. 2011-03-11 2011 Fraley v. Facebook, Inc. was a class-action case that alleged that Facebook had misappropriated users' names and likenesses in advertisements.

The apps used an HTTP referer that exposed the user's identity and sometimes their friends' identities.

The data collected was not hidden by the user's privacy settings. Bowes said he published the data to highlight privacy issues, but Facebook claimed it was already public information.

The DAB is one of the largest pro-Beijing political parties in Hong Kong. The affected groups have since been restored.
